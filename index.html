Ence Esports

ENCE is Finland’s premier esports organization originally launched in 2013, housing players in Counter-Strike: Global Offensive, Starcraft II, Hearthstone and PLAYERUNKNOWN’S BATTLEGROUNDS. Our mission is to build winning culture in to Finnish esports and over the years we’ve managed to create multiple success stories. Most recently Joona “Serral” Sotala became the World Champion in Starcraft II by winning the WCS Global Finals in Los Angeles, California and our CS:GO team had an unforgettable run to become the runner-ups of the IEM Katowice 2019 CS:GO Major. ENCE is ran by the most established Finnish esports pioneers and technology experts. We combine decades worth of experience in the esports industry including teams, players, events and marketing expertise. We are a Finnish esports organization. ENCE stands for Enceladus, one of the giants in Greek Mythology, as well as one of the moons of Saturnus. We use the motto “Ever to excel”, to do better than others, which is fundamentally what ENCE strives for: to be a role-model in the esports scene. ENCE eSports on suomalainen e-urheiluorganisaatio, joka aloitti toimintansa huhtikuussa 2013. ENCE:llä on joukkueet Counter Strike: Global Offensive, Starcraft II, PlayerUnknown’s Battlegrounds ja Hearthstone -peleissä. Kaikki seuran pelaajat ovat suomalaisia.[1][2] Huhtikuussa 2019 ENCE oli Suomen suosituin elektronisen urheilun seura HAVU:n ja Team Gigantin edellä.[3] ENCE on ottanut nimensä kreikkalaisen mytologian jättiläissoturilta Enkeladokselta. Vuosina 2013–2014 ENCE oli mukana peleissä CS:GO, Starcraft 2 ja Tekken Tag 2.[2] Huhtikuussa 2016 ENCE uudelleenperusti CS:GO- ja Starcraft 2 -kokoonpanonsa.[5] ENCE lakkautti toimintansa kesäkuussa 2014, mutta ilmoitti takaisinpaluustaan tammikuussa 2016.[6] Vuosina 2016–2017 ENCE lisäsi pelivalikoimaansa Hearthstonen ja Rainbow Six: Siegen. Vuonna 2019 ENCE perusti PlayerUnknown’s Battlegrounds-joukkueen Counter-Strike is one of the biggest esports games franchises in the history of esports. It is the 4th iteration after the original, Condition Zero and CS: Source, having being released in 2012. CS:GO brought Counter-Strike back to being one of the main stay titles in the competitive field and it is especially followed game in Finland, all of Europe and North America. ENCE came back to CS:GO in April of 2018 with a lineup named as “the best combination of young and hungry talents”. Their sole goal is to bring Finland back on the map in the game.
